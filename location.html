Location

Norway is located in Northern Europe, situated on the western part of the Scandinavian Peninsula. It shares land borders with Sweden to the east, Finland to the northeast, and Russia to the far northeast. To the west, Norway is bordered by the North Atlantic Ocean, and to the northwest, it faces the Norwegian Sea. The Arctic Ocean lies to the far north, while the North Sea stretches along its southwestern coast. The capital city, Oslo, is located in the southern part of the country, near the Oslofjord. Norway's geographical coordinates range from approximately 58 to 71 degrees north latitude and 5 to 32 degrees east longitude.

Holidays

Public holidays in Norway

  • 1 January: New Year's Day
  • March-April: Easter: Easter Sunday, and Easter Monday
  • April-June: Ascension Day
  • 25 December: Christmas Day
  • 26 December: Boxing Day

Work-free days in Norway

  • 1 May: International Labour Day
  • 17 May: Norwegian National Day
  • 24 December: Christmas Eve
  • 31 December: New Year's Eve

Commemorative days in Norway

  • 16 February: Sami National Day
  • 21 February: King Harald's Birthday
  • 23 March: Nordic Day
  • 8 May: Independence Day
  • 22 July: Official Day of Mourning

Climate

Norway's Winter, extending from December to February, transforms the landscape into a magical winter wonderland. As temperatures drop, particularly in the inland and northern regions, crisp cold air envelops the country, and snow blankets the terrain, turning it into a serene and picturesque scene. Inland areas may experience sub-zero temperatures, ranging from -5 to -20 degrees Celsius (23 to -4 degrees Fahrenheit), while coastal regions benefit from milder conditions influenced by the North Atlantic Drift.
Winter activities abound, with skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts flocking to Norway's renowned ski resorts, such as Geilo and Hemsedal, to navigate pristine slopes. The ethereal polar night envelops the far north, where the sun remains below the horizon for an extended period, creating an otherworldly atmosphere. Locals and visitors alike embrace the season with outdoor pursuits like dog sledding and ice fishing, reveling in the breathtaking beauty of Norway's winter wonderland. The warmth of cozy cabins, the enchanting glow of northern lights, and the camaraderie around crackling fires contribute to the charm of winter in Norway.
Norway's Spring, spanning from March to May, heralds the awakening of nature after the winter slumber. As temperatures gradually rise, typically ranging from 0 to 10 degrees Celsius (32 to 50 degrees Fahrenheit), the landscape undergoes a remarkable transformation. Snow begins to melt, revealing the earth beneath, and a burst of color emerges as flowers bloom, painting the scenery with vibrant hues. The coastal regions experience milder temperatures, while inland areas witness the gradual thawing of frozen landscapes.
Spring in Norway is a time of renewal and rejuvenation. The extended daylight hours allow for more outdoor activities, and hiking enthusiasts eagerly explore trails to witness the gradual return of life to the mountains and valleys. It's a season of contrasts, where remnants of winter coexist with the promise of warmer days. Fjords reflect the changing skies, and waterfalls swell with the melting snow, creating a dynamic and captivating spectacle. Norwegians embrace spring with a sense of anticipation, savoring the delicate beauty of blossoming cherry and apple trees and the sound of rushing streams as the country transitions into a season of growth and vitality.
Norway's Summer, spanning from June to August, unfolds as a season of enchantment, where the country bathes in the glow of the midnight sun. Days lengthen, and temperatures range from a pleasant 15 to 25 degrees Celsius (59 to 77 degrees Fahrenheit), creating idyllic conditions for outdoor pursuits. Along the coast, cool sea breezes provide respite, while inland areas experience warmer temperatures, making it an ideal time for hiking, biking, and exploration.
The fjords come alive with activity as tourists and locals alike embark on scenic cruises to witness the majesty of these natural wonders. The coastal towns, such as Bergen and Oslo, bustle with festivals, outdoor concerts, and vibrant street life. Norway's vast wilderness becomes a playground for adventure enthusiasts, offering opportunities for kayaking, fishing, and camping beneath the perpetual daylight. Summer also brings a profusion of wildflowers, turning meadows into a riot of colors. It's a season of celebration, where Norwegians embrace the warmth and beauty of their surroundings, reveling in the fleeting magic of Norway's summer.
Norway's Autumn, stretching from September to November, paints the landscapes with a rich tapestry of red, orange, and gold as the temperatures gradually cool. During this season, temperatures range from 0 to 15 degrees Celsius (32 to 59 degrees Fahrenheit), bringing a crispness to the air and setting the stage for the spectacular display of fall foliage. The mountains and forests transform into a picturesque tableau, offering a scenic backdrop for hiking enthusiasts.
Autumn is harvest time in Norway, with orchards and fields yielding a bounty of apples, berries, and other seasonal produce. The cozy ambiance of cafes and cabins beckons as locals and visitors seek warmth from the cooling temperatures. In the northern reaches, the aurora borealis, or Northern Lights, becomes more visible as the nights grow longer. The season is marked by cultural events, such as food and music festivals, celebrating the country's traditions and natural beauty. As Norway transitions into autumn, it invites contemplation and reflection amidst the vibrant hues, creating a captivating atmosphere that bridges the warmth of summer with the serenity of winter.
